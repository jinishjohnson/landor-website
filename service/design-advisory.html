Design Advisory

Operability-focused design reviews that identify maintainability, access, safety, lifecycle, and readiness risks before they are built into delivery.

Overview

Reviewing Design Through an Operator's Lens

Design is often reviewed for delivery compliance. AssetLens reviews design for operability, asking whether what is being designed can be maintained, operated, and managed effectively once the project is complete.

Most lifecycle risk is built in early, through access provisions, plant room sizing, maintainability decisions, and operational flow. AssetLens identifies these risks at each design stage, while they are still practical and cost-effective to resolve.

Reviews are structured, output-focused, and delivered as decision-ready reports, not generic observations.

Key Support Areas

What Design Advisory Covers

🔩

Maintainability & Access

Review of access provisions, maintenance routes, plant room sizing, replacement logistics, and servicing requirements, ensuring these are resolved at design stage, not discovered during operations.

🛡️

Safety Considerations

Review of safety-related design elements from an operational perspective, including access for inspection, emergency provisions, and ongoing compliance implications.

⚙️

Systems Strategy & Operability

Assessment of systems design from an operational standpoint, evaluating whether design decisions support efficient and cost-effective operation across the asset lifecycle.

📊

FM Operations Requirements

Review of key FM requirements including parking calculations, waste management strategy and collection areas, and operational space adequacy within the overall design.

📋

Handover Readiness Implications

Identification of design decisions that may create handover challenges, including asset data requirements, documentation implications, and operational preparation needs.

🔗

Stakeholder Coordination

Structured comment matrices to support clear communication between review findings, design teams, and the developer, with closure tracking for each submission cycle.

Benefits

The Value of Early Operability Review

Risks Identified Before They Are Built In

Design stage is the most cost-effective point to identify and resolve operational risk. Early advisory helps prevent issues from being built into delivery, where they become more expensive to correct.

Design Reviewed for Operation, Not Just Delivery

Reviews are conducted through an operator's lens, evaluating how the asset will be managed and maintained, not only whether it meets delivery requirements.

Structured Output, Not Open-Ended Commentary

Each review produces a structured report with prioritised findings, practical recommendations, and a comment matrix to support clear decision-making and resubmission tracking.

Prevents Repeated Issues Across Projects

Findings are captured and structured to reduce repeated design issues across future projects, building practical lessons from real delivery experience.
