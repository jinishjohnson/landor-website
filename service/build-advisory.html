Build Advisory

Post-award support through technical submittals, RFIs, and shop drawing reviews to protect operability intent during delivery and reduce future handover risk.

Overview

Protecting Operability Intent During Construction

Design intent can be eroded during construction. Decisions made at submittal and RFI stage, often under delivery pressure, can compromise access, serviceability, and operational performance without review from an operational perspective.

AssetLens Build Advisory provides post-award support through review of technical submittals, contractor RFIs, and selected shop drawings, assessing their impact on operability, maintainability, and future handover quality.

The goal is to protect the operability intent established at design stage before handover pressure builds, not to duplicate the role of the project manager or engineer of record.

What Happens Without Build Advisory

🔴

Operability Compromised

Delivery-stage decisions can compromise access, coordination, serviceability, and operational performance without review from an operational standpoint.

🔴

Handover Issues Built In

Problems that could have been identified at submittal or RFI stage only become visible at handover, when they are more costly and time-consuming to resolve.

Gaps in Acceptance Standards

Developers rely on contractor delivery without a clear standard for physical acceptance of areas and systems from an operational readiness perspective.

Review Focus

What Build Advisory Reviews For

🔩

Access & Serviceability

Review of delivery-stage decisions that affect maintenance access, replacement logistics, and ongoing serviceability, before they are installed and difficult to change.

🔗

Coordination Implications

Review of shop drawings and delivery-stage coordination issues that may affect access routes, clearances, or operational areas from the perspective of those who will manage the asset.

⚡

Operational Performance

Assessment of whether delivery decisions maintain the operational performance intent established at design stage, or introduce compromises that affect long-term asset performance.

📝

Lifecycle Risk

Identification of material and equipment choices that may create lifecycle cost, replacement difficulty, or operational risk, before they are committed to delivery.

📋

Handover Quality Implications

Review of RFI decisions and delivery approaches that may affect the quality and completeness of handover deliverables, asset data, and final records.

🛡️

Safety & Compliance Awareness

Observations on delivery decisions that may affect operational safety or long-term compliance, raised as advisory input for the project team's consideration.

Benefits

The Value of Build-Phase Operability Support

Operability Intent Protected Through Delivery

The operator's perspective is maintained through the construction phase, helping ensure delivery decisions do not compromise the operational performance intent established at design stage.

Handover Issues Identified Early

Problems identified at submittal and shop drawing stage can still be resolved practically, rather than being discovered at handover when correction is more costly and time-consuming.

Independent from Contractor Interests

Reviews are conducted from the developer's and operator's perspective, not influenced by contractor delivery obligations, programme pressure, or commercial interests.

Flexible Support Model

Support can be provided on a per-item basis for targeted reviews or through a monthly retainer for ongoing advisory, depending on project complexity and urgency.
