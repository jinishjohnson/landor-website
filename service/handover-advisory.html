Handover Advisory

Structured support for handover strategy, closeout, readiness, deliverables control, and transition planning, helping align delivery teams and operators on what is ready for takeover, what still requires action, and how final asset condition is recorded before occupancy and operational acceptance.

Overview

Handover as Readiness. Not Just Documentation.

Handover is one of the highest-risk moments in any development project. It is the point where contractor closeout, regulatory approvals, deliverables, asset data, operator preparation, and occupancy readiness all need to align, often under significant schedule pressure.

AssetLens Handover Advisory provides structured support across the full handover process, helping align delivery teams and operators on what is truly ready for takeover, what still requires action, and how final asset condition should be recorded before occupancy and operational acceptance.

The approach treats handover as a readiness milestone, not a documentation exercise, supporting developers in achieving a clear, controlled, and verifiable transition from construction closeout to operational performance.

Readiness-Driven Handover Support

Why Handover Is Critical

⚠️

Incomplete Closeout

Under handover pressure, open defects can carry forward and critical systems remain unverified, creating operational risk from day one.

📂

Incomplete Asset Information

Critical asset data is handed over without the structure needed for immediate operational use, creating delays and gaps in maintenance capability.

⏱️

Delayed Operational Readiness

Teams reach handover without the documentation, coordination, and readiness needed for a stable operational start, affecting confidence and performance from the outset.

Benefits

The Value of Structured Handover Advisory

Readiness Verified, Not Assumed

Handover readiness is assessed against structured criteria, not treated as a documentation exercise where completion of paperwork is mistaken for genuine operational readiness.

Reduced Delivery Risk

Proactive identification of readiness gaps before they converge at handover reduces the risk of delayed completion, stalled occupancy, and pressure on launch and revenue timelines.

Dispute Risk Reduced

Clear, structured handover records and defect tracking reduce the ambiguity that can lead to contractor claims, disputes, and commercial exposure during the DLP period.

Stakeholder Confidence Protected

Smooth, structured handover protects developer reputation and stakeholder confidence, avoiding the erosion that occurs when readiness gaps only become visible at move-in.
